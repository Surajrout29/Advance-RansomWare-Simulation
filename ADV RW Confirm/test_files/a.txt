The Dodge Challenger SRT Hellcat is a powerful muscle car known for its supercharged 6.2L HEMI V8 engine, which produces 707 horsepower (or more in performance variants), allowing for quick acceleration to 60 mph in under 4 seconds. Key features include different key fobs to unlock varying levels of power, an optional Widebody design with larger fender flares, a range of performance-oriented interior features like paddle shifters, and modes to switch between daily driving and track performance. PerformanceEngine: The heart of the Hellcat is a supercharged 6.2L HEMI V8. Horsepower: The base model produces 707 horsepower, with higher-output variants like the Super Stock offering 807 horsepower. Acceleration: The car can achieve 0-60 mph in around 3.7 seconds. Transmissions: Available with both an 8-speed automatic and a 6-speed manual transmission. Models and FeaturesKey Fob System: Different colored key fobs control engine power, with a black key providing less power and a red key unlocking the full 707+ horsepower. Widebody Options: Models are available in standard or Widebody versions, featuring wider fender flares, larger wheels, and specialized tires. Performance Technology: It includes features like line lock and launch control for drag racing, and the SRT Power ChillerÂ® which uses the A/C system to cool the engine's intake charge. Interior and ExteriorExterior: Features include a classic Hellcat badge, functional hood snorkels on some models, and large front splitters. Interior: Offers a five-seater configuration, leather accents, a Harman Kardon audio system, and dual-zone automatic climate control. Driver Controls: The leather-wrapped steering wheel has paddle shifters for manual gear changes, voice commands, and Bluetooth connectivity. Driving Experience Versatility: The Challenger SRT Hellcat is designed to be a capable daily driver while also offering settings for track or drag strip use.Sound: The supercharger creates a distinctive whine, which is a characteristic feature of the vehicle.
